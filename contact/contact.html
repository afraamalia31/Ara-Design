Must Know

Interior designers work closely with contractors and architects, interior decorators usually only work for clients, or establish relationships with furniture makers and similar professionals to decorate rooms.

Besides being able to draw, an interior designer must also be able to master various programs and software that support his work such as AutoCAD, ArchiCAD and many others as written in Kompas.

As an interior designer you shouldn't be technology stutter! Quoted from kompas.com, technology is not only used in the work process but also in marketing your abilities.

An Interior Designer's job

Interior designers have a big role in creating comfortable rooms in residential homes, offices and other buildings. Just being good at drawing is not enough to become an interior designer's capital. It requires the ability to translate and realize the wishes, needs, and tastes of the client in visual form, especially three-dimensional space. Well, meeting client expectations, it turns out that the stages in designing residential homes, offices, or public buildings take a long time, you know.

An interior designer's job starts from communicating with clients to find a match, understanding client needs, collecting interior clippings, making 2-dimensional floor plans and then making 3-dimensional renderings, assisting in shopping for furniture, artwork, and others, to supervising the implementation of the interior arrangement. In designing, an interior designer must pay attention to the ease of living, disaster prevention, and security. Designers also think about the maintenance plan of the room, methods of finishing interior decorations, and interior elements (such as materials and lighting).

Roles and Responsibilities

Solve problems related to the function and quality of the interior space environment.

Prepare drawings and implementation documents related to interior design.

Create drawings and photos of design works for various purposes.

Carry out services related to interior space in a professional manner, including the preparation of space programs, design analysis, space planning, aesthetics, and periodic supervision of work in the field.
